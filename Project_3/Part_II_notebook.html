Investigation Overview - Dataset Overview and Executive Summary¶

This analysis investigates how bike-sharing usage patterns and trip duration vary across different factors such as time of day, user type, and user age.

The dataset consists of bike trip records, including duration, start time, and user information. Additional features such as hour of day and age were derived to support deeper analysis. Data cleaning steps were performed to handle missing values and remove unrealistic entries.

The analysis reveals several key insights:

  • Bike usage follows a clear daily pattern, with peaks during morning and evening commute hours.
  • Trip duration is right-skewed, with most trips being short and a few long-duration outliers.
  • Customers consistently have longer trip durations than Subscribers, suggesting different usage behavior (casual vs routine commuting).
  • Trip duration varies by time of day, with longer trips typically occurring during midday hours.
  • There is no strong relationship between age and trip duration.

These findings are supported by multiple visualizations, including bar charts, histograms (with log transformation), and heatmaps. Each visualization is carefully selected to match the data type and clearly communicate patterns and relationships.

All visualizations are polished with appropriate titles, labeled axes (with units) to ensure readability and effective communication of insights.

Bike Usage by Hour¶

This visualization shows the distribution of bike trips across different hours of the day. Bike usage peaks during morning (around 8 AM) and evening (around 5–6 PM), indicating strong commuting patterns. Usage is lowest during late night and early morning hours. The bar chart is appropriate for showing frequency across discrete time intervals and clearly highlights peak usage periods.

def plot_count(data, column, title='', xlabel='', rotation=0):
    plt.figure(figsize=(10,5))
    sns.countplot(data=data, x=column)
    plt.title(title)
    plt.xlabel(xlabel)
    plt.ylabel('Count')
    plt.xticks(rotation=rotation)
    plt.show()
    
plot_count(df, 'start_hour', title='Bike Usage by Hour of Day', xlabel='Hour of Day')

Trip Duration Distribution¶

This visualization illustrates the distribution of trip durations. The distribution is right-skewed, with most trips being short and a small number of long-duration outliers. A log transformation was applied to better visualize the spread of the data. A histogram is suitable here as it effectively shows the distribution and skewness of the variable.

Average Trip Duration by Hour and User Type (Heatmap)¶

This visualization shows how average trip duration varies by hour of the day and user type. Customers consistently have longer trip durations than Subscribers, especially during midday hours. Subscribers show shorter and more stable trip durations throughout the day. The heatmap is appropriate for displaying relationships between multiple variables and allows easy comparison across time and user groups.
